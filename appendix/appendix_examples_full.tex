\noindent\textbf{Approved} (comment \texttt{oazic70}, author \texttt{user\_anon\_002}):

\noindent\emph{Post title:} Jumping on a moving train vs. the roof: why do I land in the same spot inside but it feels different...

\begin{lstlisting}
Context (preceding comment):
This is why hot air balloons surprise people who take a trip in them. You move with the wind so it doesn't feel like you're moving and there's no wind blowing on you or at least no feeling of a breeze.

Very calm method of travel.

Comment:
There is a similar kind of effect on a sailboat, but a little more interesting due to the wing effect of the sail.  You "feel" like you are going *extremely* fast when you are sailing close into a headwind ("close hauled", e.g., 30 degrees from the headwind), and feel like you aren't moving at all when you have the wind behind you (running with the wind), even though you are going around the same speed.  Sailboard are fastest when sailing perpendicular to the wind.
\end{lstlisting}
